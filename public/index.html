What is IoT?

The Internet of Things (IoT) is essentially everyday objects getting a mini-computer makeover. With sensors and internet connection, these "smart things" can gather data (like temperature or movement) and talk to each other. This allows them to automate tasks and improve our lives.

How does it works?

The Internet of Things (IoT) turns everyday objects into mini-communicators. With sensors built-in, they collect data like temperature or movement. Then, they send this data wirelessly to a central hub or the cloud. Here, the data is analyzed to trigger actions or provide insights, like a smart thermostat automatically adjusting heating or a fitness tracker showing your progress. Essentially, IoT creates a network of smart devices that work together for a more automated and data-driven world.

Technologies used in IoT

The Internet of Things (IoT) creates a network of everyday objects that have been enhanced with sensors and internet connectivity. These "smart devices" can sense their environment, communicate with each other, and take pre-programmed actions. Here are the necessary things or technologies used in IoT:

Sensors

These are the tiny devices embedded in IoT devices that collect data from the surrounding environment. Sensors can detect a wide range of things, such as temperature, pressure, motion, light, and sound.

Data processing
